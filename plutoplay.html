PlutoPlay Case Study

Goal:

The goal for PlutoPlay was to allow users to transfer money, budget and more without the need of a debit/credit card or going to a physical bank.

Role:

My role in this project was UX Designer, UX Researcher, and UI Designer.

Duration:

The Duration of this project consisted of nine months.

My Process

Before doing anything, we need to understand the problem and how we can solve it.

Problem Statement

Our wallet app users need a way to easily accesss their money for payments or transfers while also feeling safe when using the application.

We will know this to be true when we receive feedback on the usability of the application and if users continue to use the application on a regular basis.

Research

Competitive Analysis

During the initial stages of research I conducted a Competitive Analysis to understand similar products and how they design for their users and key takeaways. During the analysis I found that Venmo keeps it simple yet engaging between users. They do not overcomplicate their transaction processes and this is something I took note of when creating my designs.

Competitive Profile: Venmo

Key Objectives:

Safe and easy social payments with friends and family.

Overall Strategy:

Send each other hassle free payments to friends and family. Being able to split payments when going out or sending each other money as gifts.

Market Advantage:

Venmo keeps it generally simple by making it a way to easily pay each other when going out. No need to split payments physically when you can do it virtually.

Marketing Profile:

Venmo targets the youth by advertising its ease of use when paying each other. It has a fun way to make transactions with being able to comment or create a description of each payment. It has been around 2009 and has always advertised itself as a way to make social payments rather than transactional.

User Research

During the recruitment process I chose a mix of family, friends, and peers with an age range of 25 40 years old. Participants were chosen based on availability and knowledge on the subject. I chose this because it would lead to more detailed answers as this age group is better equipped with handling technology and using some form of virtual payments.

Research Goals:

  • Do users prefer to use money virtually or physically?
  • What features or services could pull more users in?
  • What could make more users want to use PlutoPlay?
  • Why do they use e-wallet or banking apps?

Key Findings

  • When using money all users still stressed that when it came to virtual or physical payments it was situation based. Meaning if they are somewhere physically they tend to pull out a card instead of using something like ApplePay.
  • Some users suggested that a product similar to ApplePay, but also with combined features from Venmo could pull users in.
  • Users expressed that if PlutoPlay was easy to use and familiar it would bring people to continue using it.
  • Users use e-wallet or banking apps to track their money easily and to also pay quicker and more efficiently.

Ideation

User Personas

When creating these User Personas I decided to create two Design Personas. They are based on the research I obtained through my user research and are a mix of my participants. I wanted to display two completely different users and their lifestyles and choices.

Rebecca

Occupation: Nurse

Lives: Union City, CA

Hobbies: TV, Tennis

"I usually stay home most of the time because I'm pretty busy, but I like to catch up with old friends when I'm free."

About

Rebecca is a new nurse with about 1.5 years of experience. She works during the night and are usually long hours. Since she's so busy she usually stays at home. She always shops online and tends to purchase a lot of food online.

Needs and Goals

  • Needs quick money transfers from wherever she is.
  • Wants to know her personal information is safe and whenever she makes a purchase or transfer.

Motivations

  • Convenience of having all her information and features she wants is there.
  • Knowing she can pay and transfer all in one app.

Frustrations

  • Difficulty keeping track of money with all her purchases
  • Can't budget accordingly due to spending a lot on food.

User Journeys

After creating the user personas and based on feedback, I was able to create journey maps. They are based on understandings as well as trying to highlight any pain-points a user might have. The two scenarios are based on comments and suggestions made when having some form of e-wallet application. I wanted to display the versatility that PlutoPlay can have for users.

Rebecca - Scenario

Rebecca one day was fed up with all her banking apps. She heard from a friend about this new app called PlutoPlay. She was told she can have all her bank accounts linked up to it and can manage all her transactions and payments. She decided to take a look and wanted to see if that was true.

Michael - Scenario

Michael noticed he uses ApplePay way too much. He realized that he found it way too easy to spend money when he doesn't have to take anything out. He found this application that acts similar to ApplePay, but allows him to budget his spending.

Site-Map

Simplicity and familiarity was the approach when creating the site-map. Based on feedback from users, I didn't want anything overly complicated and only wanted features that deemed beneficial to users. This would be used to provide organization on how PlutoPlay would be designed.

Wireframe

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Based on the site-map I created, I was able to create some low-fidelity wireframes. This was the skeleton and layout of how I envisioned how I wanted PlutoPlay to look and function as. These are the three main functions of PlutoPlay in low-fidelity; Budget, Transactions, and Banking.

Budget Wireframes

For Budget I wanted a simple dialog where users can choose what options and how much of a budget they wanted to keep track of.

Banking Wireframes

For Banking I wanted to give the user the option between adding a card manually or logging into their bank. I did this for personal preference for the user and convenience.

Transactions Wireframes

For Transactions I wanted users to be able to see their past transactions to be able to track it as well as see a familiar layout when conducting a transaction.

Transactions Wireframes Continued

During these wireframes, users have to go through a security verification to send the money. This was put here as I found during my research that users felt safer if there was a form of security around making transactions.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Going from Low-Fidelity to Mid-Fidelity I was able to incorporate more details and more structure on how everything would look like. However, the overall structure and look of the Low-Fidelity wireframes were kept as it made the most sense to users and nothing was really confusing.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

When gaining feedback from Mid-Fidelity Wireframes, changes were made. UI Design issues were corrected based on grids and spacing. Material Guidelines were incorporated as well for all wireframes. And feedback from peer led to design changes such as when sending and requesting money. It was made much clearer than before with the addition of two separate buttons to clearly indicate what action was being made.

Test

Usability Tests

With a total of six participants, I was able to conduct remote moderated tests. With the use of Zoom, participants were able to screen-share their computer while they were able to use my phone app prototype of PlutoPlay. With this, I was able to gather their feedback and gain information and understand what was good and bad about the prototype. I was successful in achieving a prototype that felt familiar with ease of use. However, participants were able to let me know that I missed some key functions such as linking a bank account to the budgeting feature.

Goal:

The goal of this study is to assess the learnability for new users or familiar users interacting with a banking application. I would like to observe and measure how well users understand the app, how useful and easy it could be for their daily use, and how efficient it can be when asked to complete basic functions such as sending money to another user.

Methodology

The study will be held at the participants' homes and will be conducted as moderated remote tests. The test will include a short briefing, and a three scenario task performance with PlutoPlay using either the mobile or desktop app version.

Participants

The study will test six participants who were recruited through personal network. They were screened for basic demographic information to ensure they fit with the user persona of PlutoPlay

Test Objectives

  • Determine if participants can locate where to send or request money to other users. Is it easy for them or overcomplicated?
  • Observe how users create a budget and understand its use from their first time interaction. Is more explanation needed?
  • When sending money or adding a card or bank account, does the user feel safe to input their personal information?
  • Observe how users navigate and find information about their transactions. Do they understand the main transactions page?
  • Obtain as much feedback as possible from participants on their overall understanding and opinion of the app's value. Would they consider using this app over other banking applications they currently have.

Affinity Map

An Affinity Map was used to collect valuable insight on the usability tests. Participants expressed praise on familiarity and ease of use, but also showed dislike in security and confusion on key features.

Rainbow Spreadsheet

Rating errors based on the Jakob Nielsen's Scale was used to create a Rainbow Spreadsheet. Possible solutions were identified and used in creating further improvements on prototype. Based on the errors found I was able to add solutions to problems found by other users.

  • Linking a card to Budget feature to determine how the budget was being tracked.
  • Adding an edit button in Profile feature to allow the user to customize their homepage.
  • Give a clearer description on onboarding to minimize confusion for users, especially for Banking feature.
  • Adding labels to icons on navigation bar so users do not have to guess what those icons mean.
  • Move security authorization to being of application to reduce repetitive actions and make app more secure.

Design

With the use of the Material Guide Principles, I am able to define all assets that PlutoPlay will use moving forward.

Visually I wanted PlutoPlay to feel calm and cool. The main colors of PlutoPlay are muted, but not dull and the typography choices are fun yet serious. I wanted PlutoPlay to have some color and not just another e-wallet app that sticks to a white and black approach.

Tone of Voice and Language

With PlutoPlay being a banking application, its voice is human. It maintains a familiar, friendly, and straightward language similar to as if you are talking to a bank teller or a friend. With this language in mind, we want to make it easy and simple for our users to navigate the app with ease.

Accessibility

  • Text is legible. All fonts used must be clear and legible for all types of users.
  • Layout is simple and clean. Screens are not cluttered with information and spacing betweens objects are adequate.
  • All icons and buttons are recognizable.

Conclusion

PlutoPlay currently is my project for the CareerFoundry UX Design course. PlutoPlay is still a work in progress and continuing on I can say I have a new hypothesis. Through the process of working on this product, PlutoPlay felt like an application that's entering a world of more experienced older siblings. With a very competitive market something needs to stand out to compete.

Hypothesis: To increase user usage and retention, we believe that adding a specific PlutoPlay card feature that uses a user selected bank account or debit/credit card, we will achieve a rise in user usage and retention.

Validation: Conduct user surveys and observe user feedback to determine if having a PlutoPlay card would positively impact the app.

I have learned through this project that:

  • Testing your product is very important and getting feedback is very valuable. Others can find gaps or major errors that maybe yourself wouldn't even notice at first.
  • UI is also very important for UX. When it comes to UI, users also pay very much attention to that and it can hurt the experience of a user if UI isn't accessible.
  • While security measures are important to users, ease of use is something that is slightly more important. Too much security can hurt the user's experience.
